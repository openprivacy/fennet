<h1>About this Stream of Consciousness</h1>

<p>In the beginning... I sold my first computer program — a copy of the Lunar Lander game in Focal — at age twelve. My thesis at MIT was <em>NewsPeek: a Knowledge-based Open Access News and Information Retrieval System</em> which (AFAIK) was the first electronic personalized (newspaper) media. Since then, I have been designing and implementing systems that enable personalization while supporting the right to privacy. Some recent forays into the field include:</p>

<ul>
  <li>In 2000, I co-founded <a href="http://openprivacy.org/">OpenPrivacy</a>, working to create a level playing field in the online digital marketplace;</li>
  <li>In 2002, I began work with the <a href="http://idcommons.net/">Identity Commons</a> as their Chief Technology Officer; and</li>
  <li>In 2004, I co-founded <a href="http://2idi.com/">2idi</a>, which created the world's first i-names i-broker.</li>
</ul>

<p>In 2005 I joined <a href="https://civicactions.com/">CivicActions</a>, an online consulting and services company helping non-profits and NGOs reach their goals.</p>

<p>There is yet another passion that drives me: my family. I met Elaine in 1998 and we had our son Steven in 2001 — what incredible joy!</p>

<h3>What will I write about?</h3>

<p>This is a stream of consciousness about the possibilities of technology and social media in the hands of the empowered masses. It started with my thesis at M.I.T. and now I'm living it. Call me the father of personalized media — though Steven prefers plain ol' Dad. And if there's a dose of political commentary and common sense thrown in for good measure, well, it's much needed.</p>
