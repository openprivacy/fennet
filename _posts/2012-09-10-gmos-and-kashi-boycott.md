<p>I&#39;d like to think <a href="/blog/2012/09/10/rachel-carson-and-silent-spring">Ms. Carson</a> would be an advocate against Genetically Modified Organisms (GMO) creeping into our food supply. There&#39;s great debate about whether Monsanto&#39;s GMO corn is unsustainable and there are reports that GMO foods can &quot;<a href="http://tampa.cbslocal.com/2012/06/19/new-study-shows-that-genetically-modified-corn-is-dangerous-jeffrey-smith-elizabeth-dougherty-food-food-nation-radio-network-audio-monsanto/">can significantly impact the viability of human cells</a>&quot;. So until we know more, at the very least it seems reasonable that GMO foods on our store shelves should be labled, which is the goal of California&#39;s <a href="http://www.naturalnews.com/036833_GMO_labeling_YES_on_37_California.html">Proposition 37 &quot;Right to Know&quot;</a> measure.</p><p>I have long been a fan of Kashi Go Lean cereals, both because it tastes good and because of the company&#39;s stated committment:</p><blockquote><p>&quot;We believe the food system needs to evolve. As one of the largest food brands in the natural and organic marketplace, we are in a unique position to make a sizeable, positive impact.&quot; (from <a href="http://www.kashi.com/ourcommitment">David DeSouza, general manager of Kashi</a>)</p></blockquote><p>So when I found out that Kashi contains GMOs and (albeit indirectly, as they are owned by Kellogg, which) has contributed $612,000 to defeat Proposition 37 - I joined a <a href="http://www.naturalnews.com/036834_Kashi_Larabar_boycott.html">global boycott</a> and returned my unopened box of Kashi to the store I had bought it from.</p>
