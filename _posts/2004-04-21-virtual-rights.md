<blockquote>
        any comments you may have on Virtual Rights and Virtual Identity. 
        <p>
        Details on: <a href="http://www.nephridium.org/virtualrights/">
        http://www.nephridium.org/virtualrights/</a>
        </p><p>
        Virtual Rights:
        <ol>
        <li>To have a Virtual Identity</li>
        <li>Not to have a Virtual Identity</li>
        </ol>
        </p>
</blockquote>

The project looks fascinating, but I'm under such a deadline that I don't have time to look at it much now, nor even read the paper.  First thoughts: I think of one's rights more like:

<ol>
<li>right to have access to profile data others keep e.g., bank, credit card, DMV, grocery club card, ... <i>(this step only needed until one gains control over their ID)</i></li>
<li>ability to assert control over usage of one's profile (we're building a mechanism to do this now using the new OASIS standards XRI and XDI</li>
<li>right to speak freely and without fear of retribution (via anonymous or pseudonymous nym)</li>
<li>ability to assert ownership (authenticated) over self-created pseudonymous data</li>
</ol>

There's a lot more to say on this, but here are some links for further reading:
