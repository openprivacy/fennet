At the first <a href="http://www.civicactions.com/">CivicActions</a> "off-site" in Amsterdam, we created a vision for ourselves and for the world.  As a new father and provider for my small family, and as someone who has unfortunately never broken into the real estate market, I was pleased that this item made the list with general consensus: <i>"every member owns their home or has the ability to"</i>.

It seemed like an impossibility just two years ago, yet today our mortgage on a home in Pittsburgh, Penn. was approved.  Granted, we're not buying a house in San Francisco (where a friend bought a single garage spot for $185,000 to go with his purchase of half of a duplex a few years ago) but we're exceedingly happy!  We extensively researched where to buy over the last few years with three primary criteria:

<ol>
<li>Excellent public schools</li>
<ul>
<li>Pennsylvania ranks <a href="http://morganquitno.com/edrank06.htm">10th in the country</a> and Mt. Lebanon, the township where we will reside, ranks <a href="http://www.postgazette.com/pg/07056/764840-298.stm">fourth in the state</a></li>
</ul>
<li>Affordable real estate</li>
<ul>
<li>for just $50,000 more than that garage I mentioned above, we're getting a <i>two car garage</i> attached to a four bedroom house with full basement and a yard large enough for badminton on a dead-end road just a two-block walk to school on a tree-lined, quiet street;</li>
</ul>
<li>Proximity to a good sized city/college town for culture, technology, etc.</li>
<ul>
<li>Mt Lebanon is six miles south of Pittsburgh, has beautiful, tree lined streets and all children walk to their neighborhood schools;</li>
<li><a href="http://www.bloomberg.com/apps/news?pid=newsarchive&sid=angA.h0Dr6Ys">Google has opened a research office</a> at <a href="http://www.cmu.edu/">Carnegie Mellon University</a> (and has a very cool <a href="http://www.hcii.cs.cmu.edu/M-HCI/2006/SocialstreamProject/index.php">social media</a> project underway);</li>
<li>Pittsburgh has <a href="http://www.gbapgh.org/casestudies_main.asp">more green "LEED-certified" buildings</a> than any other city in the U.S.</li>
</ul>
</ol>

I am honored to work with the people of CivicActions as we help our clients create our shared vision of a better world.

I feel blessed that CivicActions' vision has enabled my family to achieve its dream.
